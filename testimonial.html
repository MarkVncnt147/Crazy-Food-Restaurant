Our Clients Say!!!

This restaurant was a very homey place amidst the old town feel of Boac, and the clientele it attracts adds to the ambience of folksy familiarity. The interiors are rich with antiques and bric a brac to keep the conversations going while having the signature coffee. You should try the kari-kari, a dish that looks like curdled pork blood, and the talangkanin, together, their flavors were complimentary just like the locals told me.

Despite very few staff, I find this restaurant the finest in the whole of Marinduque.
FOOD: 4.5/5 Their Pork sisig is the best!
SERVICE: 3/5 I understand their reasons to have only one staff in their place, but this place has potential. They can offer more exicting menus as well as offer entertainment once tourism in the island increases.
AMBIANCE: 4/5 The old "Bahay na Bato" feel, this what gives the restaurant its character. I hope they could have LP records playing there instead of a CD.
OVERALL: 4/5 I will surely return to this place together with my family and friends.

kylorenze

Their menu offers yummy local dishes that you can only taste in Marinduque. Their kare-kare is like dinuguan and very tasty. Manakla is their local one-claw shrimp cooked like sinigang. Kagang is their local crab cooked in gata. All are delicious and unique. Kusina is a place to relax and reminisce life during the Spanish era. So do not rush, as there is just one gracious lady serving everyone. Take your time and take pictures as you wait for your food. Or sit by the window and watch the scenes outside.
